### 1. What is National Output and How is it Measured?
- **Gross Domestic Product (GDP):** The market value of all final goods and services produced within a country in a given time period.
    - Real GDP vs. Nominal GDP
    - GDP per capita

### 2. What Causes Economic Growth?
- Investigation of factors that drive increases in national output over time (technology, capital accumulation, labor force growth).

### 3. Why Do Economies Fluctuate?
- Study of business cycles: expansions (booms), contractions (recessions), peaks, and troughs.

### 4. What Determines Inflation and Unemployment?
- Analysis of causes and consequences of rising prices (inflation) and joblessness (unemployment).

### 5. How Do Policies Affect the Economy?
- The role of government (fiscal policy) and central banks (monetary policy).
