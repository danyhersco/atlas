- **Monetary policy** is managed by central banks using tools such as open market operations, the discount rate, and reserve requirements.
- The core goal is to influence the money supply and interest rates to achieve macroeconomic objectives.
- Lowering interest rates generally stimulates economic activity, while raising them restrains it.
- There are practical limits to what monetary policy can do, especially in cases of very low rates or high uncertainty.
- Understanding the transmission mechanisms of monetary policy is crucial for analyzing macroeconomic outcomes.
