Credit is the ability to access goods, services, or money now, with the promise to pay later. Credit plays a significant role in personal finance by offering flexibility (e.g., making large purchases, emergencies) but also carries risk if not managed responsibly.

Common uses of credit include:
- Buying a car or home
- Paying for education
- Managing cash flow between paychecks
- Building a credit history

### Benefits and Risks of Using Credit

**Benefits:**
- Convenience (no need to carry cash)
- Ability to handle emergencies
- Opportunity to build a credit history
- Access to rewards or incentives (credit card points, cashback)

**Risks:**
- Overspending beyond your means
- Accumulation of debt and interest
- Late fees and penalties
- Damage to credit score if mismanaged
