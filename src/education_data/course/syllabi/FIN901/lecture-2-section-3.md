Expenses are the costs incurred to acquire goods and services, or to fulfill obligations. Understanding where your money goes is just as important as knowing how you earn it.

### 1. Fixed Expenses

- **Definition:** Costs that do not change in amount and are paid regularly.
- **Examples:**
    - Rent or mortgage payments
    - Car payments
    - Insurance premiums
    - Subscriptions

### 2. Variable Expenses

- **Definition:** Costs that differ in amount and often in frequency.
- **Examples:**
    - Groceries
    - Utility bills (electricity, water)
    - Entertainment
    - Clothing

### 3. Discretionary Expenses

- **Definition:** Costs for non-essential items.
- **Examples:**
    - Eating out
    - Vacations
    - Luxury goods

### 4. Periodic or Irregular Expenses

- **Definition:** Costs that occur less frequently but can be planned for.
- **Examples:**
    - Car repairs
    - Annual medical checkups
    - Holiday gifts
