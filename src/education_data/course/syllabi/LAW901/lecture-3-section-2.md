### Functions

1. **Organizational Function:**  
   Constitutions create and organize the main institutions of government and allocate powers among them.

2. **Limitative Function:**  
   They limit governmental powers to protect rights and prevent abuses.

3. **Legitimating Function:**  
   Constitutions justify the legitimacy and authority of the government.

4. **Symbolic Function:**  
   They serve as a symbol of national identity and shared values.

### Forms

- **Written Constitutions:** Codified in a single text or several related documents.  
  *Example: The Indian Constitution is a comprehensive written document.*

- **Unwritten Constitutions:** Derived from multiple sources including statutes, judicial decisions, conventions, and traditions.  
  *Example: The United Kingdom operates with an unwritten constitution.*

- **Flexible Constitutions:** Can be amended easily through regular legislative processes.  
  *Example: The UK constitution.*

- **Rigid Constitutions:** Require special procedures for amendments, often involving supermajorities or referenda.  
  *Example: The US Constitution requires a two-thirds majority in Congress and ratification by three-quarters of the states.*
