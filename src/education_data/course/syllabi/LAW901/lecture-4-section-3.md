### Legislative Branch

**Function:** Law-making

- Typically comprises two chambers (e.g., U.S. Congress: Senate and House of Representatives; UK Parliament: House of Commons and House of Lords).
- Responsible for enacting statutes, approving budgets, and overseeing the executive.

### Executive Branch

**Function:** Law-implementation and enforcement

- Led by the head of government (President, Prime Minister, etc.).
- Administers public policy, executes laws, manages foreign affairs, defense, and bureaucracy.

### Judicial Branch

**Function:** Law-interpretation

- Composed of courts, headed by a highest court (Supreme Court, Constitutional Court).
- Resolves disputes, interprets laws and constitutions, checks the exercise of public power.
