In the past lectures, we've developed essential tools for measuring macroeconomic performance such as GDP, inflation, and unemployment. Today, we introduce one of the central frameworks for analyzing the economy as a whole: the Aggregate Demand and Aggregate Supply (AD-AS) model. 

The AD-AS model extends earlier concepts to explain:
- How national output (GDP), price levels (inflation), and unemployment are jointly determined.
- The causes of short-run macroeconomic fluctuations (the business cycle) and long-run growth.
