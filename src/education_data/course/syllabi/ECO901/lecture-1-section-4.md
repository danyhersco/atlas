**This course will cover:**
- Measurement of aggregate economic activity (GDP, inflation, unemployment)
- Economic growth theory and empirical evidence
- Analysis of business cycles
- Role and implications of government policies
- International dimensions (globalization, trade, exchange rates)

By the end of the course, you should be able to:
- Understand and compute basic macroeconomic indicators
- Analyze causes and impacts of economic growth and fluctuations
- Evaluate policy effects on the macroeconomy
- Connect macroeconomic theory to real-world outcomes
