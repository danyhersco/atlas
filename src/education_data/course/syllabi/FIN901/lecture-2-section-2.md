Understanding where your money comes from is the first step in managing it effectively. "Income" refers to any financial resources earned or received, which can be used to fund expenses and achieve financial goals. Letâ€™s look at the principal sources:

### 1. Earned Income

- **Definition:** Money received from working, either as an employee or a business owner.
- **Examples:**
    - Salaries and wages
    - Overtime pay
    - Tips/commissions
    - Freelance or consulting fees

### 2. Investment Income

- **Definition:** Money received as a return on investments.
- **Examples:**
    - Interest from savings accounts
    - Dividends from stocks or mutual funds
    - Rental income from property
    - Capital gains from the sale of assets

### 3. Passive Income

- **Definition:** Earnings from ventures in which you are not actively involved.
- **Examples:**
    - Royalties from books or patents
    - Income from online businesses where initial setup work is done

### 4. Transfer Payments and Other Sources

- **Definition:** Money received not in exchange for goods or services, but as a redistribution of resources.
- **Examples:**
    - Government benefits (social security, unemployment)
    - Child support or alimony
    - Gifts or inheritances
